Accessibility of Digital Teaching Materials for Students with Visual Impairment

Public sector PhD on accessibility of digital teaching materials, conducted with Statped and OsloMet, funded by the Norwegian Research Council.
March 2026 · Stephen Simei Kimogol

This project is a public sector PhD conducted in collaboration between Statped and Oslo Metropolitan University (OsloMet). The project is funded by the Research Council of Norway under its public sector PhD scheme.

The overall aim of the project is to investigate how digital teaching materials (DTMs) can be designed, developed, and used to better support elever med synsnedsettelse in primary and secondary education. The research is situated at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI), accessibility, and pedagogy, with a particular focus on bridging the gap between technical accessibility requirements and pedagogical practices.

The project adopts a mixed-methods research design structured in three phases:

  1. Barrier identification: Empirical investigation of technical, pedagogical, and usability barriers experienced by students, teachers, parents, special educators, visual consultants, publishers, and book specialists.

  2. Co-design and development: Collaborative exploration of solutions through workshops and prototyping, focusing on improving both technical and pedagogical accessibility in digital learning materials.

  3. AI and accessibility: Examination of how artificial intelligence can support teachers and content producers in creating more accessible and pedagogically effective digital teaching materials.

Conceptually, the project engages with Universal Design for Learning (UDL), Universal Design (UD), and accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG, EPUB), treating them as complementary design perspectives rather than isolated frameworks.

The project also contributes to broader societal goals related to inclusive education and aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 4 (quality education).
